What is Discograph2?

Discograph2 is an interactive visualization of the relationships between musicians, bands and labels.

All of Discograph2's data is derived from the Discogs.com discography database: nearly 9 million artists, 2 million labels, and 17 million releases creating a network of nearly 100 million different relationships.

What do all of these symbols mean?

  • Small circles represent artists.
  • Large circles represent bands.
  • Squares represent labels and other companies.
  • Solid lines show band membership.
  • Dashed lines show pseudonyms.
  • Dotted lines show other kinds of relations.

The graph only shows at most 100 entities at a time, so double-click on any circle containing a plus-sign to reveal more connections.

Discograph2 would also be impossible without the generous public data dump that Discogs.com provides monthly.

If something is not working, please file a bug report on Github.

If any artist or label information is missing or incorrect, it can be updated on Discogs.com. It may take time, even months, for the updates to be processed in Discogs, then updated here in Discograph
